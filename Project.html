Task 5

A box has 10 coins: six fair ones (prob. of a heads θ = 0.5), two unfair ones (θ = 0.25 and θ = 0.75) and two magical cheating coins (θ = 0 and θ = 1). They all look and feel the same. A coin is taken randomly from the box, and its type Θ has the distribution

θ 0 0.25 0.5 0.75 1
P(Θ = θ) 0.1 0.1 0.6 0.1 0.1

Recall the law of total probability and Bayes’ rule and solve the following problems:

1. What is the probability of flipping a heads with the unknown coin?

P(H) = P(H | θ = 0)P(θ = 0) + P(H | θ = 0.25)P(θ = 0.25) + P(H | θ = 0.5)P(θ = 0.5) + P(H | θ = 0.75)P(θ = 0.75) + P(H | θ = 1)P(θ = 1) = (0 * 0.1) + (0.25 * 0.1) + (0.5 * 0.6) + (0.75 * 0.1) + (1 * 0.1) = 0.5

$$OR$$
In [ ]:
Type <- c(0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1)
Probability <- c(0.1, 0.1, 0.6, 0.1, 0.1)

headProbability <- sum(Probability * Type)

print(paste("Probability of flipping a head: ", headProbability))

[1] "Probability of flipping a head:  0.5"

2. You flip the coin and get heads. Call this event H. Conditioned on H, what is the probability that the coin has Θ = 0.25?

P(Θ = 0.25 | H) = P(H | Θ = 0.25) * P(Θ = 0.25) / P(H) = 0.25 * 0.1 / 0.5 = 0.05

$$OR$$
